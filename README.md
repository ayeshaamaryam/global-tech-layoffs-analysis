 🌐 Global Tech Layoffs Dashboard (2020–2026)

 Project Overview
 An interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing tech industry layoffs worldwide from 2020 to 2026, covering the pandemic era, post-COVID correction, and AI-driven restructuring period.

 📊 Key KPIs
| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| 💼 Total Employees Laid Off | 1.46M |
| 🏢 Companies Affected | 2.9K |
| 📅 Peak Year |2023 |
|⚠️ Worst Sector | Retail |

 📈 Dashboard Features
 
- Global Tech Layoffs over Time — Trend line showing how layoffs evolved year by year.
- Top 10 Companies — Bar Chart showing Companies with the highest layoffs.
- Layoffs by Country — Geographic breakdown of impact.
- Layoffs by Industry — Which sectors were hit hardest.

 Filter
 
  Year Filter — Slice data by any specific year.
  
 💡 Key Insights
 
• 2023 recorded the highest number of tech layoffs during the 2020–2026 period with.

• Retail was the hardest hit industry with approximately 175K employees laid off.

• The United States experienced the highest concentration of layoffs globally.

• Amazon reported the highest layoffs among the top companies analyzed.

• 2021 showed the lowest layoff count compared to other years in the dataset.

• Layoff trends declined after 2023, indicating signs of market stabilization.
